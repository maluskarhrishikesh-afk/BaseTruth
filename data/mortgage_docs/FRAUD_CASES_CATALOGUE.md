## Original Cases (001–050)

### case_001–015: Tampered

These cases were generated with one of four tamper types applied at random:

| Tamper Type            | Description                                                        |
|------------------------|--------------------------------------------------------------------|
| `income_inflated`      | Payslip gross is multiplied 1.40–1.75× above actual bank credits  |
| `employer_fake`        | Employer CIN is replaced with an invalid/fictional value           |
| `circular_funds`       | Large same-day debit+credit pair in bank statement (round-trip)    |
| `backdated_employment` | Join date shifted back 1–5 years in the employment letter          |

> **Note on case_008 (backdated_employment bug):**
> Prior to the fix applied in `src/basetruth/analysis/structured.py` and
> `src/basetruth/analysis/packs/mortgage.py`, the `backdated_employment`
> tamper type was **not detected**.  The root cause was:
>   1. `structured.py` was not extracting `letter_issue_date` from employment letters.
>   2. `mortgage.py` had no check comparing `letter_issue_date` vs `join_date`.
>
> The fix adds `letter_issue_date` extraction + the `employment_backdating_signal`
> rule that flags when the letter is dated many years after the claimed join date
> (or impossibly, before it).

### case_016–050: Clean

No fraud applied.  All validation checks should pass (or return only low-severity
informational signals).  These are the true-negative baseline cases.

## Targeted Fraud Cases (051–060)

Each of these cases has exactly **one fraud pattern** applied.  They are intended as
regression fixtures: if a specific detection rule regresses, the corresponding case
will flip from `tampered=detected` to `tampered=undetected`.

### case_051 — `ifsc_account_mismatch`

**What happened:**  
The applicant has a valid HDFC Bank account (IFSC: `HDFC0001234`) that matches
the payslip's bank details.  However, the bank statement submitted belongs to a
different account at **ICICI Bank** (IFSC: `ICIC0009999`).  The salary credits
in the ICICI statement happen to match the declared salary, but the account does
not belong to the applicant.

**Fraud surface:**  
The applicant is likely using a sibling/friend's bank statement to show higher
average balance or to hide adverse transactions in their own account.

**Detection rule:** `bank_ifsc_account_consistency`  
The IFSC prefix on the bank statement (`ICIC`) does not resolve to the same
institution as payslip bank name (`HDFC Bank`).

**Documents tampered:**
- `bank_statement.pdf` — IFSC and bank name changed to ICICI.

### case_052 — `salary_cross_doc_mismatch`

**What happened:**  
Three sources disagree on the applicant's salary:

| Source              | Stated monthly gross |
|---------------------|---------------------|
| Payslips (3 months) | ₹1,44,000           |
| Bank salary credit  | ₹83,333             |
| Employment letter CTC| ₹1,00,000/yr → ₹83,333/mo |

The payslips are inflated ~73% above the actual salary.

**Fraud surface:**  
Higher payslip gross → higher declared income → larger loan eligibility.
The bank statement honestly shows the real credit from the employer.

**Detection rule:** `reconcile_income_documents` (service layer)  
Payslip annual gross (₹17,28,000) exceeds bank credits × 12 (₹9,99,996) by
>15%.  Also payslip gross exceeds employment letter implied monthly by >15%.

**Documents tampered:**
- `payslip_2026_jan.pdf`, `payslip_2026_feb.pdf`, `payslip_2026_mar.pdf` — gross inflated.

### case_053 — `bank_date_range_fabricated`

**What happened:**  
The bank statement header claims the period `01-Oct-2025 to 31-Mar-2026`
(6 months), but all transaction dates fall within **Jan–Mar 2026** only (3 months).
The October–December 2025 section was removed from the statement, likely because
it contained:
- Dishonoured cheques / returned payments
- Unusually low balances below minimum requirements
- Unexplained large outflows

**Fraud surface:**  
Banks require 6 months of statements.  Submitting a 3-month statement with a
forged 6-month header allows the applicant to withhold unfavourable transaction
history while technically appearing to comply.

**Detection rule:** `statement_date_range_validity`  
The earliest actual transaction date (01-Jan-2026) does not fall within 5 days
of the stated period start (01-Oct-2025).  The gap is ~3 months.

**Documents tampered:**
- `bank_statement.pdf` — header date range is fabricated.

### case_054 — `bank_address_mismatch`

**What happened:**  
The bank statement IFSC code is `HDFC0001234` (identifies **HDFC Bank**), but the
statement header names the institution as **State Bank of India, Delhi Main Branch**.
This is an impossible combination: each IFSC is unique to a single financial
institution.

**Fraud surface:**  
The applicant altered the bank name in the statement header (easiest to edit in a
PDF) but forgot to change the IFSC code, leaving an inconsistency that is trivially
detectable by the IFSC registry.

**Detection rule:** `bank_ifsc_account_consistency`  
IFSC prefix `HDFC` resolves to `HDFC Bank` in the IFSC registry; statement header
says `State Bank of India`.  Names are inconsistent.

**Documents tampered:**
- `bank_statement.pdf` — bank name in header changed; IFSC left as HDFC.

### case_055 — `bank_arithmetic_error`

**What happened:**  
The 5th row (0-indexed) of the running-balance column has been inflated by
**₹1,00,000**.  All subsequent balances forward-carry this phantom credit,
making the account appear ₹1 lakh richer than it actually is.

**Fraud surface:**  
Inflating the running balance allows the applicant to meet the lender's minimum
average monthly balance (AMB) requirement for loan eligibility without actually
holding those funds.

**Detection rule:** `debit_credit_total_arithmetic`  
For each row: `previous_balance + credit − debit ≠ stated_balance` at row 5
(delta = ₹1,00,000).  Sequential balance consistency check fails.

**Documents tampered:**
- `bank_statement.pdf` — row 5 balance inflated by ₹1,00,000.

### case_056 — `salary_structure_low_basic`

**What happened:**  
The payslip shows a severely distorted salary structure:

| Component       | Fraudulent   | Normal (expected) |
|-----------------|-------------|-------------------|
| Basic           | 10% of gross | 30–50%            |
| HRA             | 70% of gross | 20% of gross      |
| Special Allowance | remainder | ~20%              |

**Why this is fraud:**  
- Basic salary is the basis for EPF, Gratuity, and Leave Encashment.  
  Minimising basic reduces the employer's (and employee's) PF obligation.
- HRA at 70% of gross vastly exceeds the Section 10(13A) exemption ceiling
  (50% of basic for metro cities = 5% of gross here).  
  The excess HRA is taxable but declared exempt, reducing TDS.

**Detection rules:**
- `basic_gross_proportion`: basic (10%) < minimum threshold (30%).
- `hra_proportion_of_gross`: HRA (70% of gross = 700% of basic) >> max 50% of basic.

**Documents tampered:**
- `payslip_2026_jan.pdf`, `payslip_2026_feb.pdf`, `payslip_2026_mar.pdf`

### case_057 — `hra_exceeds_basic`

**What happened:**  
HRA (₹15,000) is **75% of basic** (₹20,000).  Under the Income Tax Act
Section 10(13A), the HRA exemption cannot exceed:
- 50% of basic salary (metro city), or
- 40% of basic salary (non-metro).

The excess HRA (₹5,000/month = ₹60,000/year) is being incorrectly declared
tax-exempt, reducing TDS liability.

**Detection rule:** `hra_proportion_of_gross`  
HRA / basic = 75% > the 60% internal warning boundary.  Also triggers
`hra_exceeds_basic_statutory` advisory.

**Documents tampered:**
- `payslip_2026_jan.pdf`, `payslip_2026_feb.pdf`, `payslip_2026_mar.pdf`

### case_058 — `extreme_backdating`

**What happened:**  
The employment letter (dated **15 March 2026**) states the applicant's date of
joining as **01 January 2000** — a gap of **26 years**.

The applicant's actual join date (per payslips) is **01 June 2022** (4 years ago).
The employment letter date was backdated to project the image of a long-tenured,
stable employee — a criterion many banks use for loan assessment.

**Note:** This is the extreme version of the `backdated_employment` pattern
that was missing detection before the case_008 fix.

**Detection rule:** `employment_backdating_signal`  
`letter_issue_date (2026-03-15) − join_date (2000-01-01)` = 26 years, far
exceeding the `_BACKDATING_THRESHOLD_YEARS = 2` threshold.  
Also: payslip join date (2022-06-01) contradicts letter join date (2000-01-01).

**Documents tampered:**
- `employment_letter.pdf` — join date backdated to 01 January 2000.

### case_059 — `pan_mismatch`

**What happened:**  
The PAN number on payslips (e.g., `ABCDE1234F`) **differs** from the PAN on
Form 16 (e.g., `XYZAB9876G`).

In a legitimate bundle, the PAN must be identical across all documents for the
same individual.  A mismatch indicates either:
- The Form 16 was borrowed from a different person (identity fraud), or
- The payslips were fabricated for the loan applicant but Form 16 belongs to
  the actual employee at that company.

**Detection rule:** Cross-document PAN consistency (service layer)  
`payslip.pan ≠ form16.pan` → signals `pan_cross_document_mismatch`.

**Documents tampered:**
- `form16.pdf` — PAN replaced with a different randomly-generated PAN.

### case_060 — `gift_letter_loan_disguise`

**What happened:**  
The gift letter declares a gift of **₹18,00,000** (90% of the property value
of ₹20,00,000) from a family member for down-payment purposes.

The same amount appears as a **large NEFT inflow** in the bank statement on
10-Feb-2026, labelled `NEFT/GIFT TRANSFER - FAMILY`.

**Why this is suspicious:**  
A genuine gift should come from the donor's own savings and represent a
reasonable portion of the purchase price.  When the gift:
1. Covers 90%+ of the total purchase price, AND
2. Appears in the bank statement as an incoming transfer (not as existing savings)

...then the 'gift' is likely a **back-channel loan** that will need to be repaid
but is not disclosed as a liability in the loan application.  This hides the
true debt-to-income ratio.

**Detection rule:** `gift_amount_vs_property_value`  
`gift_amount / property_value = 90%` > the warning threshold (typically 50%).
Also: bank statement shows large inflow matching gift amount within 30 days.

**Documents tampered:**
- `gift_letter.pdf` — gift amount set to 90% of property value.
- `bank_statement.pdf` — large incoming NEFT transfer injected.
